# Skill: Alonuko Ltd — Complete Business Understanding

## Company Profile

Alonuko Ltd is a **luxury bridal fashion house** in London. Founded by Gbemi Okunlola (Creative Director). The business designs, produces, and sells bespoke and made-to-order wedding dresses, evening wear, and accessories. Revenue comes from consultation fees, dress sales (deposits → balance payments), alterations, and occasional ready-made/wholesale.

**Revenue model: Project-based.** Each bride is a project generating 3–5 invoices over months (consultation → deposit → fittings → balance → alterations). A single client may appear as 3+ Shopify transactions. Client count = unique brides from Monday.com confirmed clients board, NOT invoice/transaction count.

**FY: April–March** (UK standard). Data period: Apr 2025–Mar 2026.

## MONTHLY TREND ANALYSIS

The business has a clear pattern:
- **Apr 2025:** Loss of £132K — heavy COGS (opening stock loaded), low revenue
- **May 2025:** Best month — £232K revenue, £47K GP, but still lost £48K after OpEx
- **Jun 2025:** High GP% (85%) — low COGS month, likely timing of stock purchases
- **Jul–Sep 2025:** Revenue plateau £97–187K, losses £13–66K/month
- **Oct–Dec 2025:** Revenue declining (£141K → £67K), losses widening (£19K → £93K)
- **Jan–Mar 2026:** Revenue collapse (£68K → £39K), losses £74–99K/month

**The trend is alarming.** Revenue has fallen 83% from peak (May: £232K → Mar: £39K). If this continues, the business has weeks, not months.

**Possible explanations:**
1. **Seasonal** — Bridal orders placed 6–12 months before weddings. Spring = peak ordering. Winter = low season. Revenue should recover Apr–Jun 2026.
2. **Pipeline exhaustion** — Marketing isn't generating enough new brides. Check Monday board for new enquiry dates.
3. **Delivery backlog** — Revenue is recognized on delivery, and WIP is building (closing WIP > opening). Dresses may be in production but not yet delivered → revenue delayed.
4. **Data sync issue** — Mar 2026 shows zero Bridal MTO revenue but only Shopify Sales. Check if Xero invoices for Mar are posted.

**Cross-reference needed:** Monday confirmed clients board dates, Klaviyo email metrics, and Shopify order dates to distinguish seasonal vs structural decline.
